About

Hi, I'm Nick. I work with founders and data/AI teams who need AI actually running in production, not just demoed in a deck. The hard part is usually the last mile, where real data and a security review decide whether it survives.

I studied electrical and computer engineering at UCLA, which taught me to think in systems and constraints before features. That led me into solutions architecture, the seat between what a business needs and what actually ships. At Tonic I owned the technical sale end to end. Lately most of my time goes to one hard problem at a time, building Gostly.

I'm based in Ewa Beach, Hawaii, and work remotely with teams anywhere. I keep the schedule deliberate and guard time away from the keyboard, because that's where the energy for the hard problems comes from. When I'm not working, I'm usually in the water.

Most AI projects die in the gap between the demo and production. I live in that gap.

Currently building → Gostly: deterministic, in-tenant testing for AI agents in regulated environments.
