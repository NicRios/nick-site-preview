About

Hi, I'm Nick. I work with founders and data/AI teams who need AI shipped into production — not slideware, not a demo that breaks the moment it meets a security review.

I started in electrical & computer engineering at UCLA, where I learned to think in systems and constraints before features. That pulled me into solutions architecture — the seat between what a business actually needs and what actually ships. At Tonic I owned the technical sale end to end; these days I spend most of my time forward-deployed inside one hard problem at a time, building Gostly.

I run all of this from Ewa Beach, Hawaii — remote-native and intentional about time. I work async, price on outcomes instead of hours, and protect the balance on purpose, because that's what keeps the judgment sharp over a long project. Off the keyboard, you'll find me in the water.

Most AI projects die in the gap between the demo and production. I live in that gap.

Currently building → Gostly — deterministic, in-tenant testing for AI agents in regulated environments.
